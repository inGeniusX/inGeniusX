1: Background

Local

In 2018, co-founder Andrew Tran started to realize the educational inequalities in his community.

Andrew's high school had just changed it's name from J.E.B. Stuart High School to Justice High School to reflect the diversity of the community. There was a new school mascot, new principal, and new attitude, but there was one thing that didn't change. The bleak educational circumstances that existed previously persisted despite the new changes. Andrew's true epiphany came when he witniessed classmates not being able to afford backpacks on the first day of school, when he found out that over 65% of his peers were on the free/reduced lunch program, and when he realized the boy next to him in class was an immigrant struggling to keep up with school while making ends meet.

Global

In 2018, co-founder Rayan Yu had only heard of the extreme impoverishment in rural countries.

To find out and assist on his own, Rayan pioneered a two month volunteering mission to the rural county of PingJiang, Hunan, China, a location of significant poverty for many families and children. When he got there, he witnessed firsthand: the accounts he had heard of were more than true. Students were studying under depressingly destitute conditions, with little to no progress at all. Throughout the trip, Rayan personally visited students homes to assist in their education, experiencing the true ordeals of countless childrens' lives. After personally tutoring, living with, and helping countless students for 8 hours a day, Rayan understood how fragmented opportunities truly were for such an unimaginable portion of the global youth.

Sharing a personal connection with education on a global and local level, Rayan and Andrew sought to create a more holistic set of educational opportunities for those around them.
